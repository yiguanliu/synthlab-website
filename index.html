The problem

The data you need most is the
data you have the least of

To train a system to spot a fault, you need examples of it. But faults are rare. That's what makes them faults. Waiting to collect enough real ones costs time, money, and risk you can't get back.

Months to capture enough real footage of one rare fault
Manual labelling: slow, costly, and inconsistent
Limited access to sensitive sites and equipment

Our approach

So we build the world ourselves

Instead of waiting to find a fault in the wild, we build a realistic, controllable version of where it happens, and generate the data straight from it.

Study the real thing

We capture how the real environment looks and behaves: its geometry, materials, and lighting.

Rebuild it, flexibly

We recreate it as a controllable scene, adjusting conditions and faults to create endless realistic variety.

Generate, pre-labelled

Every image arrives already labelled, because the scene knows the answer before the picture is taken.

Where we focus today

Catching the fault before
it becomes a problem

We focus on one of the hardest problems in computer vision: teaching a system to recognise the faults that are too rare, varied, or costly to photograph enough of in real life.

  • Every fault, on demand

    Any defect, any severity, any background: generated as often as needed, instead of waited for.

  • Every condition, under control

    Lighting, angle, dirt, motion, weather: all adjustable, so training covers the full range of reality.

  • Answers you can trust

    We build the scene, so there's no guesswork. Labels are always exactly correct.
